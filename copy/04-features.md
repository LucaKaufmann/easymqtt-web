# 04 — Features grid

Grid of five feature cards. On desktop the first two cards are wide
(half-width each), and the next three are one-third width. On mobile
the grid collapses into a horizontal carousel — each card becomes a
slide. "iOS‑native" uses a non-breaking hyphen (U+2011).

---

### Section kicker

Small uppercase label above the section title.

> Features

---

### Section title (H2)

"nothing you don't." is set in *italic serif* for display emphasis.

> Built for real MQTT work, *not just demos.*

---

### Section lede

Intro paragraph below the title.

> EasyMQTT gives you the workflows that matter on mobile: secure broker access,
> live topic monitoring, fast publishes, reusable favorites, and glanceable dashboards
> on the Apple devices you already carry.

---

### Card 1 — Connect (wide)

Tag pill in the top-left of the card, then H3, then body.

> Tag: **Connect**
>
> ### Connect securely to real brokers
>
> Save brokers with MQTT 3.1.1 or 5.0, raw MQTT or WebSockets, TLS/SSL,
> imported PKCS#12 client certificates, and per-broker credentials. Switch sites in a tap.

---

### Card 2 — Subscribe (wide)

"pretty‑printing" uses a non-breaking hyphen (U+2011).

> Tag: **Subscribe**
>
> ### Inspect every message as it arrives
>
> Subscribe with wildcards, save favorite topics, filter by QoS and retained state,
> and inspect JSON payloads with readable formatting when you need to debug fast.

---

### Card 3 — Publish (third-width)

> Tag: **Publish**
>
> ### Publish commands without friction
>
> Send one-off messages or save repeatable commands as favorites. Set QoS and retain,
> then fire off device actions, scene changes, test payloads, or Zigbee2MQTT commands in seconds.

---

### Card 4 — Widgets (third-width)

> Tag: **Widgets**
>
> ### Turn topics into mobile dashboards
>
> Build graph and multi-graph widgets for sensor data like temperature, humidity, energy,
> and production metrics. See the numbers that matter without reopening the app.

---

### Card 5 — Locations (third-width)

> Tag: **Locations**
>
> ### Pick the right broker for the place
>
> Save where a broker is usually used, keep a connection history on device,
> and let Suggested Broker surface the nearest saved broker when you're on site.
